# Product Directory Submission Guide

Strategic plan for submitting Recruiting Compass to product discovery platforms to drive early user acquisition.

---

## Priority Directories

### TIER 1 - Must Submit (Highest Traffic & Impact)

#### 1. Product Hunt

**Platform:** Product Hunt (producthunt.com)
**Audience:** Tech-savvy early adopters, startup community
**Traffic Potential:** 1,000-5,000+ visits on launch day
**Best For:** Generating initial momentum and social proof

**Submission Details:**

- Account: Create free account
- Listing Title: "Recruiting Compass - Organize your college recruiting"
- Tagline (60 chars): "Free platform to manage schools, coaches & timeline"
- Description: 500-1,000 words highlighting problem/solution/features
- Thumbnail Image: 500x500px (logo or product shot)
- Gallery Images: 5-10 high-quality screenshots
- Pricing: "Free" (with optional premium note)
- Category: "Education" or "Productivity"
- Website URL: recruitingcompass.com
- Social Links: Twitter, LinkedIn

**Launch Strategy:**

- Submit early morning EST (midnight PT = 3 AM PT) to maximize trending time
- Use "launch" featured image immediately
- Prepare 500-word launch post for comments
- Have founder/team ready to engage in comments
- Plan for 12+ hours of active monitoring

**Submission Timeline:** Day 1 (Friday morning)

**Success Metrics:**

- Target: 50+ upvotes
- Target CTR: 5%+ from Product Hunt
- Bonus: Featured (top of page)

**Contact:** No email needed, just launch

**Content to Include:**

- Personal founder story (why you built this)
- Problem you're solving
- How it's different from alternatives
- Early traction/feedback
- What's next
- How they can help

**Submission Timeline:** Day 1-2

**Success Metrics:**

- Target: 200+ views
- Target: 20+ comments
- Goal: Build community around product

**Link:** indiehackers.com/forums/show

### TIER 2 - Should Submit (Good Traffic)

#### 5. Hacker News

**Platform:** Hacker News (news.ycombinator.com)
**Audience:** Tech-savvy developers, entrepreneurs
**Traffic Potential:** 200-3,000+ visits
**Best For:** Technical credibility and developer audience

**Submission Details:**

- Account: Create free HN account (username, password only)
- Title: "Show HN: Recruiting Compass - Organizing college recruiting"
- Must start with "Show HN:" to be in Show HN category
- Submission: Direct link to product (recruitingcompass.com)
- Description: NOT used by HN, goes in comments by you
- Category: Auto-selected (Show HN)

**Strategy:**

- Submit early morning EST (6-8 AM EST typically best)
- Post detailed comment immediately with problem/solution
- Be ready to engage in comments (12+ hours)
- Answer questions authentically
- Don't be overly promotional

**Comment Template:**

```
Hi HN! This is [Your Name], founder of Recruiting Compass.

We built this because [problem statement].

Currently [early traction/users].

We'd love feedback on [specific question].

[Link to docs/help]
```

**Submission Timeline:** Day 1-2

**Success Metrics:**

- Target: 100+ upvotes (rare but possible)
- Target CTR: 2-5% from HN
- Goal: Developer feedback and credibility

**Guidelines:**

- No spam/marketing language
- Be genuine and transparent
- Answer all questions
- Don't upvote yourself (HN will know)
- Follow community guidelines

## Submission Content Strategy

### Title & Tagline Formula

**Formula:**
"[Product Name] - [One sentence benefit]"

**Examples:**

- "Recruiting Compass - Organize your entire college recruiting journey"
- "Recruiting Compass - From chaos to clarity in college recruiting"
- "Recruiting Compass - Free platform to manage your recruiting"

### Description Template (500-1000 words)

```
## Problem Statement (150 words)
[Describe the pain student-athletes and families face]
- Juggling multiple apps and email
- Losing track of coach interactions
- No clear timeline or milestones
- Family coordination challenges

## Solution Overview (200 words)
[Introduce Recruiting Compass]
- Problem we solve
- How we solve it
- Key differentiators

## Key Features (150 words)
1. School List Management
2. Interaction Tracking
3. Recruiting Timeline
4. Parent Collaboration
5. Smart Suggestions

## Who It's For (100 words)
- Student-athletes (14-18)
- Parents
- Coaches (future)

## Why We Built It (150 words)
[Founder story - why this problem matters to you]

## Current Traction (50 words)
[Beta users, early feedback, signups]

## What's Next (100 words)
[Roadmap and future plans]

## Try It Free (50 words)
[CTA with link]
```

### Screenshot Strategy

**Each directory needs 3-5 high-quality screenshots:**

1. **Dashboard** - Overview showing main value
2. **Schools List** - Core feature with visual design
3. **Timeline/Phases** - Unique feature
4. **Interaction Log** - Another key feature
5. **Mobile View** - Mobile responsive

**Screenshot Requirements:**

- 1200x800px or 1024x768px (check each directory)
- PNG or JPG (PNG preferred)
- High quality (no pixelation)
- Realistic demo data
- Clear feature visibility

## Maximizing Directory Impact

### Product Hunt Specific

**Pre-Launch (Thursday before):**

- Prepare launch post (500 words)
- Prepare response templates
- Gather team for launch day
- Prepare social media posts
- Brief team on talking points

**Launch Day (Friday):**

- Submit early morning (3 AM PT / 6 AM ET)
- Post comment immediately (5 minutes after)
- Engage actively for first 4-6 hours
- Respond to all comments/questions
- Thank voters and supporters
- Share social posts every 2-3 hours
- Monitor trending position
- Prepare for sudden traffic spikes

**Post-Launch (Saturday):**

- Continue engagement
- Collect testimonials
- Share metrics
- Thank community
- Answer follow-up questions

### General Directory Success Tips

**For all directories:**

1. **Be responsive** - Answer all questions within 24 hours
2. **Be authentic** - Share real story, not marketing speak
3. **Be helpful** - Provide genuine value in responses
4. **Show traction** - Share early metrics and user feedback
5. **Engage community** - Upvote/comment on others' products
6. **Follow guidelines** - Read and follow each platform's rules
7. **Use data** - Share early results, testimonials, metrics

## Common Submission Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Don't:**

- Be overly promotional or salesy
- Make false claims about features
- Ignore comments and questions
- Spam the same description everywhere
- Use low-quality screenshots
- Over-promise features
- Break community guidelines
- Self-upvote or manipulate
- Disappear after launch

✅ **Do:**

- Be authentic and transparent
- Answer all questions
- Customize descriptions per platform
- Use high-quality visuals
- Underpromise and overdeliver
- Respect community norms
- Engage actively for 24+ hours
- Show early traction and feedback
- Build relationships with community
