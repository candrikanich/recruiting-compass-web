# Recruiting Compass Glossary

A reference guide for recruiting and app-specific terms used in the Recruiting Compass platform.

---

## A

**Account Linking**
Connecting a parent account to a student-athlete account to enable shared visibility and collaboration. Parents can view timelines, schools, and interaction history without having full editing access.

**Athlete (Student-Athlete)**
The primary userâ€”a student currently playing competitive baseball and exploring college recruiting options. Athletes create and manage their recruiting profile.

---

## C

**Coach**
A college baseball coach at a target school. Tracked individually with contact information, communication preferences, and interaction history.

**College Tier**
A classification of colleges based on competitive level:

- **D1 (NCAA Division I)**: Highest competitive level
- **D2 (NCAA Division II)**: Mid-tier competitive colleges
- **D3 (NCAA Division III)**: Smaller colleges, no athletic scholarships
- **JUCO (Junior College)**: Two-year colleges with strong baseball programs
- **NAIA**: Independent four-year colleges

---

## D

**Dashboard**
Your home page after logging in. Shows overview of your recruiting progress including active schools, upcoming timeline tasks, recent interactions, and performance metrics.

**Document**
Any file related to recruiting: film/highlights video, academic transcripts, recruiting survey responses, or correspondence with coaches. Upload and organize documents within the app.

---

## E

**Email Template**
Pre-written emails for common recruiting scenarios (introducing yourself, following up after a showcase, requesting a meeting). Customize and send directly to coaches.

**Event**
A recruiting-related activity you plan to attend or have completed: showcase, tournament, campus visit, coach visit, or recruiting expo. Track dates, locations, and coach attendance.

---

## F

**Fit Score**
A data-driven assessment of how well a school matches your academic, athletic, and personal goals. Calculated from:

- Academic fit (GPA/test scores vs. school standards)
- Athletic fit (your stats vs. team roster)
- Location preference
- Program performance
- Scholarship availability
- Coaching philosophy alignment

Range: 1-10 (10 = perfect fit)

---

## I

**Interaction**
Any communication or meeting with a coach:

- Email exchange
- Phone call
- Text message
- Coach visit to your high school
- Camp or showcase attendance
- On-campus visit
- Zoom call

Log interactions to track responsiveness and build a timeline of your relationship with each coach.

**Interest Calibration**
A quick questionnaire (6 questions) you complete after each coach interaction to rate your interest level in that school. Helps the app track your changing interests over time and suggest relevant next steps.

---

## L

**Log (Interaction Log)**
Your complete record of all interactions with coaches. Helps you:

- Remember when you last spoke with a coach
- Track responsiveness
- Prepare for future conversations
- Identify coaches who aren't engaged

---

## M

**Milestone**
A major recruiting goal or deadline in your timeline (e.g., "Attend summer showcases," "Receive first offer," "Make college decision").

---

## P

**Parent Account**
A secondary account for a parent/guardian. Can be linked to a student-athlete account to:

- View the recruiting timeline and progress
- See all coach interactions
- Receive notifications about important milestones
- Collaborate on decisions

**Phase** (Timeline Phase)
A recruiting stage in your 4-year timeline:

- **Freshman Phase**: Foundation building, skill development
- **Sophomore Phase**: Active outreach, tournament attendance
- **Junior Phase**: Peak recruiting period, showcases, visits
- **Senior Phase**: Narrowing choices, official visits, decision

**Priority Tier**
A classification of schools based on your interest level:

- **Reach Schools**: Dream schools (long shot, high standards)
- **Target Schools**: Realistic matches (good fit, competitive)
- **Safety Schools**: Likely admits (strong fit, good chance)

---

## R

**Recovery Plan**
A personalized action plan created when a coach goes silent (no response for 30+ days). Suggests specific actions to re-engage (timing of follow-up, new communication angle, etc.).

**Responsiveness Score**
A metric tracking how quickly and frequently a coach responds to you. Helps identify engaged vs. non-responsive coaches.

---

## S

**School**
A college or university you're targeting for recruiting. Tracked with:

- Basic info (name, location, division level)
- Team roster and coaching staff
- Academic requirements
- Scholarship info
- Your fit score
- All coaches at that school

**Showcase**
An organized baseball event where scouts/coaches view multiple athletes. Commonly used for recruiting evaluation.

**Suggestion**
AI-powered recommendations generated based on your profile, interactions, and preferences. Examples:

- "Follow up with Coach Smith (last contact 45 days ago)"
- "Add new schools matching your fit score criteria"
- "Schedule campus visit at Target School A"

---

## T

**Template** (Email Template)
See "Email Template"

**Timeline**
Your 4-year recruiting roadmap from freshman through senior year. Shows:

- Phase-appropriate tasks and milestones
- Critical deadlines
- Progress tracking
- Suggested actions for each phase

**Task**
An action item in your recruiting timeline. Examples:

- "Update your recruiting profile"
- "Send introduction email to Coach A"
- "Attend at least 2 summer showcases"
- "Complete recruiting survey for School X"

Tasks are phase-appropriate and unlock as you progress through the timeline.

---

## U

**Unsigned**
Status of an athlete who has not yet committed to a college. Most users in the Recruiting Compass are unsigned, actively working toward a commitment.

---

## V

**Visit** (Campus Visit)
A trip to visit a college campus, typically to:

- Meet the coaching staff
- See practice and game facilities
- Tour campus and academic buildings
- Get a feel for the college environment

Visits are tracked in your timeline and can be logged as interactions.

---

## Additional Recruiting Terminology

**Athletic Recruiting Period**
Time windows when coaches can actively recruit (varies by division and sport). D1 has specific contact periods; D2/D3 have looser restrictions.

**Commitment**
When you verbally commit to attend a college and play for their baseball program. Usually occurs junior or senior year.

**Dominant Position**
Your primary defensive position(s) on the team (e.g., Shortstop, Catcher, Outfield).

**Grade Level**
Your year in high school:

- **Freshman**: 9th grade
- **Sophomore**: 10th grade
- **Junior**: 11th grade
- **Senior**: 12th grade

**Highlight Film (Highlights)**
A compilation video of your best plays showing your skills to coaches. Typically 3-5 minutes long.

**Off-Season**
Period between competitive seasons when the team trains and develops skills.

**On-Campus Visit**
See "Visit (Campus Visit)"

**Recruiting Survey**
A questionnaire sent by coaches requesting information about your academic performance, athletic goals, and interests. Coaches use this to evaluate potential recruits.

**Scout Report**
An evaluation of your abilities written by a coach or scout.

**Showcase Event**
See "Showcase"

**Strength/Speed Test**
A measurement of your physical abilities (60-yard dash, vertical jump, exit velocity, etc.).
